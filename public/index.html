Fund Mandate & Investment Thesis

Investment Thesis

Strong US companies that make steady cash and lead their markets — in cloud/AI, payments, and business software — should keep growing even with high rates, and the smartest way to own growth right now is to pay up only for real quality, not hype. The fund looks for profitable businesses with strong balance sheets and reliable, growing sales. The most expensive chip stocks are kept small, a big bank that benefits from high rates is held to steady things out, and heavily indebted infrastructure and unpredictable commodity stocks are left out.

Objective & Strategy

The fund is built to grow capital over the long run by holding a small group of strong, profitable US-listed companies. The focus is on businesses that make real cash, have a clear edge over competitors, and sit in areas that should keep growing — like cloud and AI, payments, and business software. The fund is willing to pay a higher price for these stocks, but only when the company is genuinely good quality, not just because it's popular right now. With rates staying high and expensive tech at risk of selling off this quarter, the riskiest tech positions are kept small. The fund also holds a couple of steadier names — a large, well-funded bank and one defensive stock — to soften the drops.

Risk Tolerance

High. The goal is growth and the fund can handle bigger ups and downs, but the riskiest positions are limited and some steady stocks are held, so the risk is taken on purpose, not carelessly.

Will Hold

Strong, profitable US-listed companies that should keep growing (big software, cloud/AI chipmakers, payment companies like Visa or Mastercard); a large, well-funded US bank that does better when rates are high; up to one defensive stock for stability.

Won't Hold

No single stock over 25% of the fund; expensive, hype-driven chip stocks capped at about 10% combined; no pipeline or utility companies that carry a lot of debt; no oil, gas, or mining stocks as main holdings; nothing that isn't profitable.

Diversification & Cash

7 holdings (range 6-8); 4-5 sectors; cash ~5% (never above 10%). The dashboard's Cash KPI on the Overview tab shows the live % of NAV in cash against this band.
